What is Classicism?

Classicsm is an art movement inspired by the styles and principles of ancienct Greece and Rome. However this isnt the artistic works from the Greek and Roman empires themselves. This is art produced afterwards in similar style.

Principles of Classicsm Art

  • Follows the aesthetics of classic Greek and Roman art
  • Upholds those aestheticsas the ideal
  • Incldues fine art, architecture and theatre
  • Emphasizes harmony, clarity, restraint, universality and idealism

Classicism timeline

Classicism in Architecture

Architecture was one of Classicism’s strongest expressions. Buildings were designed with symmetry, columns, domes, and precise proportions inspired directly by Greek temples and Roman public buildings.

  • The Panthéon (Paris)
  • The British Museum (London)
  • The White House (USA)
  • La Madeleine (Paris)

Classicism in Theatre

Classicist theatre followed strict rules known as the “Three Unities”: one place, one time, one main action. Plays focused on moral lessons, dignity, and controlled emotion.

  • Unity of Time: story occurs within 24 hours
  • Unity of Place: one setting
  • Unity of Action: no side plots

Spotlight Artists

Jacques-Louis David

A leading figure of Neoclassical painting, David shaped the visual language of the French Revolution through structured, moral, and dramatic compositions.

Jean-Auguste-Dominique Ingres

Known for his precise linework and refined portraits, Ingres elevated Classicism into an elegant, almost mathematical style.
